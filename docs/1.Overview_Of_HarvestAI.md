# Problem Statement
The issue is that smallholder and commercial farmers in South Africa struggle to decide what crops to plant each season, which results in often relying on experience and IoT devices that provide current metrics on the conditions of the soil (it’s pH balance, the hydration of the soil). This then leads to:

 Reduced crop yields due to having poor alignment with soil or weather conditions,

 Water and fertilizer waste are a byproduct of not optimizing the utilization of the resources based on the current weather conditions,

 Financial loss happens primarily due to not planting non-optimal crops based on the conditions of the land,

 A resulting market oversupply of certain crops occurs, while others are underproduced and causes instability and not predicable yields during harvest

# Solution
 HarvesterAI creates intelligent system that analyzes the various factors that go into the process of farming, such as:

 The pH balance of the soil, understanding what are the nutrient requirements for the current crops that would be most helpful to their growth,

 The historical data of the weather conditions in a region, where AI can have evaluations on the conditions that are most occurrent and detecting abnormalities to warn farmers in advance about abnormal conditions,

 Having a crop rotation feature that then gives insights for crop optimization features that take into consideration the seasons, historic weather conditions and plant health metrics
# Commercialization strategies
 The main means of generating revenue from this technological solution would be:
 1. SaaS subscription for farmers (tiered by farm size: R50–R500/month)
 2. Freemium model: basic crop advice free, advanced predictive analytics paid
 3. B2B partnerships with agricultural cooperatives, NGOs, and government extension programs
 4. Data monetization: aggregated regional insights sold to government or agri-businesses

# Competitors
The current competitors that our solution is competing with are the following companies:
1.AgriPredict (weather-based crop alerts)
2.Hello Tractor (farm machinery)
3.Government advisory apps (text-based, static)

# Uniqueness(Competitive Advantage)
With HarvesterAI, the uniqueness of the project stems from the fact that the 4th Industrial Revolution is based on implementing intelligence into pre-existing practices, and farming is known to be one of the most traditional practices that is reliant on a hands approach of planting, yielding and harvesting. Through integrating intelligent systems into this practice, we aim to then usher in the digital insights into the South African context of farming, where our rich natural resources of the produce of this country can have a system that accommodates for abuncance.

We are focusing on three key factors of the market:

1.The Land: 
	the integration of predictive seasonal planning with AI, where we are prioritizing abundance of produce through understanding the quality of the soil, and then having crop swapping as a core feature of the application

2.The Market: 
	having econometric functionality where farmers can understand the value of their yield through predictive analytics assists in understanding the sustainability and scalability of the swapping of the yield of the crops  

3.The Technology: 
	the use of Agentic systems that are trained on the data being produced by the farmers can assist in ha overall personalization of the user experience where they have a solution tailored to their personal soil, weather and temperature conditions as well as overall plant health


# Target Market
The primary target market:

3.Tertiary: NGOs, government agricultural programs, agricultural colleges
